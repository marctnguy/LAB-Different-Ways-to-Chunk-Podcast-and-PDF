Through this lab, we discovered that recursive character chunking was the strongest overall choice for both the PDF and the podcast transcript because it preserved structure, sentence flow, and context better than fixed-size splitting. For the PDF, recursive chunking worked especially well because it respected headings and paragraph boundaries. For the podcast transcript, it also kept the conversational flow more naturally than fixed-size chunking. Token-based chunking was still useful when precise LLM context-window control was the priority, but it was less focused on preserving document structure. Fixed-size chunking was the weakest method because it often broke sentences and ignored structure, while semantic chunking was promising but better suited as an advanced optional experiment than as the default approach.
